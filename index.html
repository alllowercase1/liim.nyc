Liim Lasalle

Liim is a Harlem-raised artist known for his genre-blurring sound that fuses hip-hop, soul, and alternative textures. Built from dissonant jazz loops, ghostly soul fragments, and loose, freeform lyricism, Liim's music carries the emotional weight and restlessness of New York City as it's actually lived in. Beginning in 2022, he released a series of singles including "Petty Pete," "Edward 40 Handz," "Mezcal," and "Why Why," leading into his debut project, Liim Lasalle Loves You.

The album unfolds like a quiet coming-of-age story. It lingers on the uncertainty of early adulthood—the shifting dynamics of relationships and the life lessons that come with growing up. His writing is confessional, carried by production that bends and reshapes itself as emotions shift.

Liim moves fluidly in NYC's creative scene between music, fashion, and visual art—modeling for Gucci, Adidas, and Supreme, while documenting his world through a camcorder lens. Liim's lyrical perspective is telling of a wisdom far surpassing his years. He is not only a musician, but a pioneering creative in New York's contemporary creative scene.
